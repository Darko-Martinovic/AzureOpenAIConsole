Belgian Fruit Pricing Guide

Belgium's fruit market offers diverse pricing across different retail channels and seasons. Understanding these price patterns helps consumers make informed purchasing decisions.

Seasonal Fruit Prices:
- Spring (March-May): Strawberries €4.50-6.00/kg, rhubarb €3.00-4.00/kg
- Summer (June-August): Cherries €5.00-8.00/kg, plums €2.50-3.50/kg, peaches €3.00-4.50/kg
- Autumn (September-November): Apples €2.00-3.00/kg, pears €2.50-3.50/kg, grapes €3.50-5.00/kg
- Winter (December-February): Oranges €2.00-2.80/kg, kiwis €4.00-5.50/kg

Regional Price Variations:
Brussels markets tend to be 10-15% higher than rural areas. Antwerp offers competitive pricing due to port proximity. Ghent farmers markets provide excellent value for locally grown produce.

Import vs Local:
Belgian-grown fruits are typically 20-30% more expensive than imports but offer superior freshness and support local agriculture. Popular Belgian varieties include Conference pears and Jonagold apples.

Shopping Tips:
- Visit markets in the last hour for discounted prices
- Buy seasonal fruits for best value and quality
- Consider bulk purchases for preserving and freezing
